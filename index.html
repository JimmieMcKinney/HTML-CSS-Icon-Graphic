Bronx School District 10 Struggles

By Jimmie McKinney

Sept. 30, 2021

Many districts in New York City's public school system struggle with student performance. Bonx's school district 10 is a prime example of this. District 10 has a math proficiency of 38 percent and a reading proficiency of 42 percent. There are many outside factors that challenge a students ability to learn. Two of the most promineint in district 10 are poverty and language conflicts. Both are added stressers students. The majority of students in the district are also minorities. The lack of quality education for these students will only perpetuate wealth inequality in the country between minorities and white americans.

Bronx school district 10 boasters a 94% minority student body. Much higher than the New York public school average of 57%. 69% of all students in the district are hispanic.


Many student are not native english speakers; 20.1% are english language learners.


Poverty affects a students ability to learn. Bronx school district 10 has one of the highest student poverty rates in the state at 82.4%.


Bronx school district 10 has a graduation rate of 76% according to 2021-21 statistics. Ranking in the bottom 50% of New York public schools.
